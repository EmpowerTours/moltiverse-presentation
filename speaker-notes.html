Q&A Quick Reference

Q&A

Top 12 predicted questions with short answers.

1. "I don't know how to code. Can I still participate?"

Yes. AI tools like Claude and Cursor can help you write code from natural language descriptions. The starter kits handle a lot of boilerplate. Many winners at hackathons are beginners who learn fast.

2. "What is Monad?"

A high-performance blockchain — think of it as a super-fast, cheap version of Ethereum. 10,000 transactions per second. EVM-compatible, so existing tools work with it.

3. "What's an AI agent?"

An AI program that can take actions autonomously — make transactions, post content, manage funds, interact with other agents. It's AI that does things, not just answers questions.

4. "Do I need to buy crypto to participate?"

No. The hackathon provides what you need to build on testnet. You don't need to invest any money.

5. "Can I participate from anywhere?"

Yes. Moltiverse is fully remote and global. Everything happens on Discord and online.

6. "What if I don't finish in time?"

Submit what you have. Rolling submissions mean you get early feedback. An incomplete but interesting project is better than no submission at all.

7. "How is vibe coding different from regular coding?"

Traditional coding: you memorize syntax and type everything. Vibe coding: you describe what you want in plain language, AI generates the code, you review and refine. It's faster but you still need to understand what you're building.

8. "What tools do I need?"

A laptop, internet connection, and free tools: Claude (AI), a code editor (VS Code or Cursor), and a crypto wallet (MetaMask). All free.

9. "How was EmpowerTours built?"

Using AI-assisted development on the Monad blockchain. Smart contracts in Solidity, frontend in React, provably fair randomness via Pyth Entropy. One person, AI tools, and persistence.

10. "Is Web3/crypto a scam?"

There are scams in every industry. Web3 technology itself — blockchains, smart contracts, decentralized governance — is legitimate and being adopted by major institutions. Focus on building real utility, not speculation.

11. "What if I want to build a team?"

Join the Moltiverse Discord. There's a team-finding channel. Post your skills and interests, find complementary teammates. Solo is fine too.

12. "What happens after the hackathon?"

You keep your project, your skills, and your connections. Many hackathon projects become startups. At minimum, you have a portfolio piece and real blockchain development experience.
